SVG Logo Tip: Make sure your SVG logo uses currentColor or defines its fill/stroke as #FFFFFF so it shows up correctly on dark backgrounds. If you have a colorful logo, use a PNG with transparent background instead.

Hero & Portrait Images

FileDimensionsRatioUsed In
hero/hero-photo.jpg960×1200px min4:5 portraitHomepage hero section (right column)
hero/about-photo.jpg960×1200px min4:5 portraitAbout page, blog single author bio

The hero image is displayed in a 4:5 portrait ratio container. Use a clean portrait photo with the subject centered or slightly left. Avoid busy backgrounds — a soft bokeh or studio background works best with the dark template aesthetic.

Recommended: Professional headshot, editorial portrait, or creative workspace photo. The template overlays floating stat cards on top, so leave some breathing room in the upper-right and lower-left areas.

What makes a great project thumbnail?

  • Show the actual interface, product, or output — not abstract graphics
  • Use consistent backgrounds (dark, light, or your brand color) across thumbnails for a cohesive grid
  • For UI screenshots: use a device mockup (browser window, phone frame) for professionalism
  • Avoid text-heavy thumbnails — let the visuals speak
  • Shoot logo/brand work on a neutral or brand-colored background

Mockup Tools: Mockuuups Studio, Smartmockups, or Figma's built-in device frames work great for UI project thumbnails.

Testimonial Avatars

FileDimensionsFormatNotes
testimonials/client-1.jpg … client-6.jpg200×200pxJPG/WebPSquare avatar photos. Displayed at 44×44px in the template.

Avatars are rendered as circles in the template. Center the subject's face in the frame. Professional LinkedIn-style headshots work perfectly here — or if a client doesn't have a photo, use their company's logo on a colored background.

If you have fewer than 6 testimonials, simply duplicate the existing cards and update the content — the images are referenced by number, so client-1.jpg can appear more than once.

Client / Brand Logos

FileFormatRecommended SizeNotes
clients/client-logo-1.png … PNG (transparent) or SVG200×80px max-heightLogos for the scrolling marquee / clients grid.

Use white or light-colored versions of your clients' logos (most brand guidelines provide these). The template renders logos at 30% opacity and removes color (grayscale) by default, brightening on hover. This creates a clean, consistent look regardless of each logo's original color scheme.

Important: Only use logos of clients/brands who have given you permission to display them on your portfolio. Many larger brands have policies about this. When in doubt, ask first or omit.

Open Graph / Social Preview Image

FileDimensionsFormat
og-image.jpg1200×630pxJPG (keep under 300 KB)

This image appears when your portfolio link is shared on Twitter, LinkedIn, Facebook, or iMessage. It should include your name, tagline, and a preview of your work — think of it as your portfolio's business card.

Design it in Figma or Canva. Include:

  • Your name and title
  • A sample of your best work
  • Your website URL
  • On-brand colors that match your portfolio

Update the og:image meta tag in every HTML file's <head> with the full absolute URL to this file:

<meta property="og:image" content="https://yourdomain.com/assets/images/og-image.jpg" />

Optimization Tips

Optimized images are the single biggest performance improvement you can make. Target these sizes:

Image TypeMax File SizeNotes
Hero portrait200 KBMost visible, compress carefully
Project thumbnails80–120 KB eachLoaded as a grid, keep lean
Blog covers100 KB each16:9, consistent compression
Testimonial avatars15 KB eachTiny display size, compress aggressively
OG image300 KB maxRequired by some platforms

Recommended Tools

  • Squoosh (free, browser-based) — best quality control
  • TinyPNG — batch compress JPG/PNG
  • ImageOptim (Mac) — drag-and-drop batch compress
  • Sharp (Node.js) — programmatic batch processing

Formats & Modern Images

The template HTML uses standard <img> tags with JPG sources by default. For even better performance, use <picture> to serve WebP with JPG fallback:

<picture>
  <source srcset="assets/images/projects/project-1.webp" type="image/webp" />
  <img src="assets/images/projects/project-1.jpg"
       alt="Project thumbnail"
       width="600" height="375"
       loading="lazy" />
</picture>

WebP typically reduces file size by 25–35% vs. JPG at the same visual quality. Convert your JPGs to WebP using Squoosh or the cwebp command-line tool.

Lazy Loading

All images below the fold already include loading="lazy" in the template HTML. This defers loading until the image is about to enter the viewport, significantly improving initial page load time. Don't add loading="lazy" to the hero image — it should load immediately.

Always specify width and height

Every <img> tag in the template includes width and height attributes. These prevent Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) by reserving the correct space before the image loads. Keep these accurate when you replace images with different aspect ratios.

Core Web Vitals: Optimize your hero image (LCP element) first — it has the biggest impact on your Lighthouse performance score. Aim for a score of 90+ on both mobile and desktop.
